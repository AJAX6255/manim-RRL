Astrophysics of RR Lyrae Variable Stars

The Kappa (κ) Mechanism

RR Lyrae stars are pulsating variables driven by the Kappa Mechanism (valve mechanism) in their helium ionization zones. As gravity compresses the stellar envelope, helium is doubly ionized, becoming more opaque (increasing κ). This blocks outgoing radiative energy, building up heat and pressure. The high pressure forces the envelope to expand. During expansion, helium recombines, opacity decreases, the radiation escapes, and pressure drops, causing gravity to pull the envelope back in.

Pulsation Modes: RRab vs. RRc

RR Lyrae variables are categorized by their pulsation modes:
• RRab stars pulsate in the fundamental mode. They feature longer periods (0.4 to 1.0 days), larger amplitudes (up to 1.5 mag), and characteristically asymmetric light curves (a very rapid rise to maximum brightness followed by a gradual decay).
• RRc stars pulsate in the first overtone. They have shorter periods (0.2 to 0.45 days), lower amplitudes, and nearly symmetric, sinusoidal light curves.

Standard Candles & Distance Ladder

RR Lyrae are key Standard Candles because they occupy a narrow region of the Horizontal Branch (the Instability Strip), resulting in a nearly constant average absolute magnitude of $M_V \approx +0.6$. By accounting for metallicity $[Fe/H]$ via the Period-Luminosity-Metallicity (PLZ) relation, we calculate a high-precision absolute magnitude $M_V$. Comparing $M_V$ to the measured apparent magnitude $m_V$ yields the Distance Modulus: μ = m_V - M_V = 5 log10(d) - 5, allowing direct distance determination to globular clusters and galaxies.
